One important aspect of this principle is that you need to learn how to use language in order to make your voice heard, to persuade people of your expertise in your future profession and to establish strong connections with colleagues, clients and other stakeholders in projects you may be involved with in the future: you don’t want to be one of the people who are silenced because they do not express their ideas in convincing ways. The first step in understanding this is to consider language register.</p><p style="text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><br/></p><p style="padding-left: 26pt;text-indent: 0pt;line-height: 113%;text-align: left;">From Grellier and Goerke (Chapter11. Principle 2: Make it formal where appropriate), the main concepts you need to understand are:</p><p style="text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><br/></p></li><li><p style="padding-left: 60pt;text-indent: -16pt;line-height: 113%;text-align: left;">The concept of language register is about the degree of formality we use when we communicate in particular situations. We can think of it as a scale running from <b>Formal </b>at one end to <b>Slang </b>at the other, with <b>Informal </b>(also called Colloquial or Conversational) in the middle.</p><p style="text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><br/></p><p style="padding-left: 33pt;text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><span><img width="538" height="106" alt="image" src="APC HUM Week 1 study guide - 2020-2/Image_011.jpg"/></span></p><p style="text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><br/></p><p class="s8" style="padding-left: 70pt;text-indent: 0pt;text-align: center;">Figure 2: Language Register</p></li><li><p style="padding-top: 4pt;padding-left: 60pt;text-indent: -16pt;text-align: left;">All communications, written and oral, belong somewhere on this scale.</p></li><li><p style="padding-top: 7pt;padding-left: 60pt;text-indent: -16pt;line-height: 113%;text-align: left;">There is no one language register that is correct in all situations, or that is better than others – we assess language register by what is appropriate rather than by what is correct. So it would be just as inappropriate for you to speak to fellow artist or writer in formal language as it would be to write a report for your manager in slang.</p></li><li><p style="padding-top: 5pt;padding-left: 60pt;text-indent: -16pt;line-height: 112%;text-align: left;">Successful language users are able to modify their register to suit all situations, without thinking about it consciously. This is called <b>code switching</b>.</p></li><li><p style="padding-top: 5pt;padding-left: 60pt;text-indent: -16pt;line-height: 113%;text-align: left;">Code switching is very important for your success at university, and then in the professional world you will inhabit. The best way for you to improve your ability to code switch is to have lots of practice using different registers, with awareness of how you are doing it.</p></li></ul><p style="text-indent: 0pt;text-align: left;"><br/></p><p style="padding-left: 26pt;text-indent: 0pt;line-height: 113%;text-align: left;">Look back at the posting you made on the discussion board.
